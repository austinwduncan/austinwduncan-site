## About This Series

Philippians is the most personally warm of Paul's letters. Written from prison, it radiates a contentment that defies circumstances. Paul's concern for the Philippian church is evident on every page — he loves these people, and they have supported him in his ministry.

The letter is held together by the theme of joy: the word appears more times per page here than anywhere else in Paul's letters. And crucially, the joy is not circumstantial. It is grounded in the person and work of Jesus Christ.

## Sessions

### Session 1: Partnership in the Gospel (1:1–11)
Paul's greeting and prayer. What does it mean to be partners in the gospel?

### Session 2: Living Is Christ (1:12–26)
The advance of the gospel through imprisonment. "For to me to live is Christ, and to die is gain."

### Session 3: Standing Firm Together (1:27–30)
The call to unity under pressure. The church as a political body with its own citizenship.

### Session 4: The Mind of Christ (2:1–11)
The great Christological hymn. Humility as the form of the gospel in community life.

### Session 5: Working Out Salvation (2:12–18)
God's work and our work. The tension that must not be dissolved.

### Session 6: Two Friends and a Warning (2:19–3:11)
Timothy, Epaphroditus, and the dogs. What Paul counts as gain versus loss.

### Session 7: Pressing On (3:12–4:1)
The upward call. Forgetting what lies behind. Citizenship in heaven.

### Session 8: Peace and Contentment (4:2–23)
Conflict resolution, the discipline of rejoicing, and the secret of contentment. "I can do all things through him who strengthens me."
