## About This Series

The five doctrines of grace — sometimes arranged under the acronym TULIP — represent the Reformation's answer to the question: who saves, and how?

They are not a comprehensive theology. They are five interlocking answers to the single question of how a sinner comes to saving faith and perseveres in it. The series handles each doctrine exegetically, with attention to the objections, the historical context, and the pastoral implications.

## Sessions

### Session 1: Total Depravity
What the Bible means when it says we are dead in trespasses and sins — and why this is actually good news.

**Key texts:** Ephesians 2:1–3; Romans 3:10–18; John 6:44

### Session 2: Unconditional Election
God chooses before the foundation of the world — not on the basis of foreseen faith, but according to his own purpose and will.

**Key texts:** Ephesians 1:3–6; Romans 9:10–16; 2 Timothy 1:9

### Session 3: Definite Atonement
Christ's death is sufficient for all and efficient for the elect. The cross actually saves — it does not merely make salvation possible.

**Key texts:** John 10:11–15; Isaiah 53:4–6; Matthew 1:21

### Session 4: Irresistible Grace
When God calls effectively, the sinner comes. The new birth precedes and produces faith. This is not coercion; it is re-creation.

**Key texts:** John 6:37–44; Ezekiel 36:26–27; Acts 16:14

### Session 5: Perseverance of the Saints
Those whom God saves, he keeps. The doctrine is not eternal security as a blank check — it is the assurance that God finishes what he starts.

**Key texts:** John 10:27–29; Romans 8:28–39; Philippians 1:6
