Austin W. Duncan serves as a pastor and teacher committed to the faithful exposition of Holy Scripture. His ministry focuses on preaching, biblical teaching, and equipping the local church for life and mission.

He holds graduate-level training in biblical studies and theology, with particular interest in Pauline studies, Old Testament theology, and the intersection of biblical scholarship and pastoral ministry.

Austin is convinced that the church grows primarily through the preaching of the Word and the administration of the sacraments — and that the pastor's first calling is to know the text well enough to set it loose on a congregation.

When he is not in the text, he is usually reading church history, drinking too much coffee, or watching college football.

## Beliefs

Austin holds to the historic Reformed and evangelical faith as summarized in the Westminster Standards and expressed in the great tradition of Protestant orthodoxy. Key commitments include:

**Scripture** — The sixty-six books of the Old and New Testaments are the inspired, inerrant, and sufficient Word of God. They are the final authority for faith and practice.

**God** — The one true and living God exists eternally in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. All three persons are fully and equally God.

**Humanity** — Human beings are created in the image of God, fallen in Adam, and in need of redemption. The fall is total in its scope: every faculty of the human person is affected by sin.

**Christ** — Jesus of Nazareth is the eternal Son of God, fully divine and fully human, who lived a sinless life, died as a substitutionary atonement for sin, rose bodily from the dead, and is now exalted at the right hand of the Father.

**Salvation** — Salvation is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone. It is the sovereign work of a triune God: the Father elects, the Son redeems, and the Spirit regenerates and seals.

**The Church** — The local church is the primary locus of Christian life and witness. It is constituted by Word, sacrament, and discipline rightly administered.

**Eschatology** — History is moving toward the return of Christ, the resurrection of the dead, and the final judgment. The new creation will be the dwelling place of God with his people.

## Values

**Exegetical rigor.** The sermon or article must first reckon with what the text says before it can say anything else. The congregation deserves a pastor who has done the work.

**Theological depth without academic inaccessibility.** Serious theology should be communicable to ordinary people. The gap between the scholar and the congregation is a failure of communication, not evidence of the material's inherent complexity.

**Pastoral presence.** Ministry is not a content-delivery service. It is the work of a shepherd who knows the sheep by name.

**Honest engagement with hard questions.** The Christian faith is not served by avoiding difficult objections or pretending the hard texts don't exist. It is better served by taking them seriously and trusting that the truth can handle scrutiny.
